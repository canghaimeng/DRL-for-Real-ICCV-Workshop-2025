Submission Guidelines for DRL for Real Competition

This competition focuses on developing and evaluating disentangled representation learning (DRL) methods on realistic datasets. Participants are expected to submit their models and results following these guidelines:

Submission Requirements

  1. Model Submission: Submit your trained model along with the code used for training and evaluation. The model should be capable of learning disentangled representations from the provided datasets.
  2. Documentation: Include a detailed description of your approach, highlighting the key innovations and how your method addresses the challenges of applying DRL to real-world data.
  3. Results: Provide quantitative results on the evaluation metrics (Disentanglement, Robustness, Interpretability, and Practical Utility).
  4. Visualization: Include visualizations that demonstrate the disentangled nature of your learned representations.

Evaluation Criteria

Submissions will be evaluated based on the following criteria:

  • Disentanglement: How well does your model separate different factors of variation in the data?
  • Robustness: How well does your model perform under different real-world conditions and perturbations?
  • Interpretability: How easily can humans understand the learned representations?
  • Practical Utility: How useful are the disentangled representations for downstream tasks?

The final ranking will be determined by the weighted sum of these metrics, with an emphasis on practical utility and real-world applicability.

Submission Format

Please submit your files in one of the following formats: .zip, .tar.gz, or through a GitHub repository link. Ensure that your submission includes all necessary code, models, and documentation as specified above.
